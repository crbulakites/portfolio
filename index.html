My name is Connor Bulakites...

..., and I created this site to serve as my canvas for artistic experimentation on the internet. I am a computer programmer who is primarily interested in deconstructing the relationship between technology and art. The site is a bit empty right now due to me having just put it up, but I intend to populate it soon with artwork, code, and perhaps some ramblings!

One annoyance I have often had with the internet is that it can be fairly hard to verify when something was written or revised unless its author was diligent enough to keep such notes and share them with you. That's why this site is tracked using Git and hosted using GitHub Pages! You can view the entire source code and revision history for this website here. Additionally, I would like to publish much of the future content on this site under free and open licenses, though I will need some time to determine which licenses are the most appropriate for each type of media.

Due to recently experiencing a bout of JavaScript fatigue, I have opted to limit this site to mainly just HTML and CSS. My justification? It's unbeatably fast, simple, compatible, and it's much harder for me to disrespect your privacy! Plus, React is fun, but I spend enough time tinkering with it at work. If I do include JavaScript, WebAssembly, or some other code that runs automatically on a page, I will notify you, disclose to you what it does, and include a link to the source code.

That seems like enough writing for tonight! Drop by sometime in the future, and perhaps there will be something else to see! Best wishes!
